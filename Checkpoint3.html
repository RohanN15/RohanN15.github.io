Predicting the Surival of Post Op Heart Failure Patients¶

Summer 2024 Data Science Project, By Rohan Nidumbur

Introduction¶

Heart Failure is one of the leading causes of death in the United States. While many patients get surgery after heart failure, some still end up dying in post-op. This tutorial will look through datasets to find specific features that might have a significant impact on the patient's likelihood of survival. Answering this question is important since it could help the many patients who experience heart failure every year.

Data Curation¶

There are various publicly available datasets which show different features of post op heart failure patients (ex. smoking, anemia, etc.) and whether they survived or not.

I am choosing two Kaggle datasets detailing medical information about patients with heart failure and whether or not they survived.

Heart_failure_clinical_records_dataset.csv - This dataset contains 13 medical features of 299 patients during their post-op care after experiencing heart failure. No DOI citation, Authors: Davide Chicco - Krembil Research Institute, Toronto, Ontario, Canada Giuseppe Jurman - Fondazione Bruno Kessler, Trento, Italy

Heart_failure_clinical_records.csv - This dataset contains the same 13 medical features of 5000 patients during their post-op care after experiencing heart failure. No DOI citation, no authors listed

I will perform a Chi2 test between the anaemia column and the DEATH_EVENT column to see if there is a correlation between patients with anaemia dying of heart failure. I will use significance of 0.05

Null Hypothesis: There is no correlation between patients with anaemia and chance of dying of heart failure

Alternative Hypothesis: There is a correlation between patients with anaemia and chance of dying of heart failure

Since the P-Value is less than the alpha value of 0.05, we can reject the null hypothesis, there is a correlation between patients with anaemia and patients dying of heart failure.

Insights and Conclusions¶

Heart Failure is a complex issue and we cannot find a way to prevent post op heart failure deaths with the data available. However, this tutorial has provided some insights into some of the features which play a significant role in the final outcome of the patient.

The data visualization shows that patients with low serum creatinine levels are more likely to survive. We also see from the chi2 test that there is a statistically significant correlation between anaemia and survival. Finally, we see that patients who are older are more likely to die. This initial data visualization gives uninformed readers an idea of how the features can impact a patient's survival chances.

We then trained four separate models on the same data to determine which one was the most appropriate for answering our initial question. This process gave readers, both informed and uninformed, some insights into which models are effective in use cases such as this one.

Finally, the plot of our feature importances shows which features had the most impact on the model's prediction. Both informed and uninformed readers could have found some interesting insights here. For instance, other than the initial correlation matrix, I did not explore the impact of time in my data visualization so that was a surprise.

Overall, this tutorial gives readers the opportunity to learn about features that affect a patient's likelihood of survival post op in heart failure and they can also see which machine learning models work the best in these types of scenarios.
